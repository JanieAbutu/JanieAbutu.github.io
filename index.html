About

My work in security comes from environments where detection quality is not abstract — it directly affects operational stability, financial integrity, and trust. In banking and fintech systems, a weak detection model doesn’t just create noise; it creates blind spots or operational disruption at scale.

I started in SOC environments working within traditional alert-driven workflows: monitoring SIEM dashboards, triaging incidents, and responding to escalations across high-volume infrastructures. That experience gave me operational depth, but it also exposed a structural issue — most detections were not designed from an understanding of attacker behaviour or system context.

Instead, they were often inherited from vendor templates, generic rulesets, or reactive tuning cycles built after noise had already appeared. This created a gap between what security teams wanted to detect and what the systems were actually capable of reliably identifying.

That gap is what pushed me toward detection engineering as a design discipline rather than an operational function. I shifted from consuming detections to reconstructing how they should be built — starting with threat behaviour, mapping it to attack surfaces, and reasoning about what evidence would exist in telemetry if that behaviour occurred in a real system.

In that transition, frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK, NIST CSF, and CIS Controls became part of my reasoning model — not as compliance artifacts, but as structured ways to define detection intent. They help bridge the gap between abstract security requirements and observable system behaviour.

From there, my focus expanded into what I see as a translation layer in security engineering: converting threat models and risk understanding into operational systems. That includes SIEM architecture, telemetry pipelines, correlation logic, enrichment workflows, and automation systems that scale detection beyond manual reasoning.

The projects in this portfolio reflect that progression — from SOC operations, to detection design, to building systems that connect security reasoning directly to operational execution.
