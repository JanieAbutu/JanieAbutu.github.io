About

My background is in environments where getting detection wrong has consequences — banking infrastructure, fintech platforms, and regulated systems where a missed alert or a miscalibrated detection at scale carries real operational cost.

That context shaped how I think. Early in my career I was doing what most SOC analysts do — monitoring, triaging, escalating. I was good at it. But I kept running into the same problem: the detections I was working with weren't built from a genuine understanding of the threat. They were built from templates, vendor defaults, and reactive tuning. The noise was the symptom. The root cause was that detection logic had been disconnected from security reasoning.

That gap is what pushed me toward detection engineering — and toward a specific way of working. Not starting with a tool and asking what it can detect, but starting with the threat, mapping it to the attack surface, identifying the telemetry that would surface it, and then engineering the logic around it. Threat modelling, risk assessment, and frameworks like MITRE ATT&CK, NIST CSF, and CIS Controls stopped being compliance exercises and became the reasoning layer I build detections on top of.

The other thing I developed was what I think of as the translation function. Security requirements don't arrive pre-formatted for a SIEM. A risk assessment surfaces a threat. A framework maps a control. Someone has to convert that into a correlation rule, a telemetry pipeline, a detection threshold, an automation playbook. That translation — from security principle into operational system — is where I spend most of my engineering effort, and where I think the real leverage is.

Every project in this portfolio starts from that position — from threat modelling and security reasoning through to SIEM architecture, telemetry pipelines, and automation workflows The SIEM architectures, the data pipelines, the automation workflows built to scale, the attack simulations with defense mechanisms — each one is an attempt to build a system that is thought through end to end.
